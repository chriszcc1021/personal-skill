# Product

## Register

product

## Users

TeamForge is designed first for a single team leader who owns one shared team across multiple concurrent projects. Team members may get their own mode later, but the current product is a leader cockpit with full visibility into people, projects, tasks, risk, and hiring gaps.

## Product Purpose

TeamForge helps a leader see how one shared team is being stretched across projects, decide where to intervene today, and understand what talent is missing for upcoming work. The product should answer: who is overloaded, which project is at risk, who can take the next task, what capability is missing, and what seniority level is needed to close the gap.

## Brand Personality

Calm, decisive, and operational. The interface should feel like a serious management instrument, not a generic project tracker or a decorative dashboard.

## Anti-references

Avoid Jira-style administrative clutter, Notion-style loose documents, decorative SaaS landing visuals, and gamified UI that hides operational signal. Avoid treating projects as isolated teams, because the real operating model is a shared talent pool across many projects.

## Design Principles

1. Shared team first: the main model is people across projects, then tasks and risks.
2. Leader action over raw data: every important surface should imply a decision, intervention, or tradeoff.
3. Separate planned allocation from actual pressure: allocation, active tasks, overdue tasks, and deadline pressure must be visible together.
4. Talent gaps must be operational: show missing function, affected projects, urgency, and recommended seniority.
5. AI is a copilot, not the product: deterministic views must work without AI, with AI used to sharpen summaries and recommendations.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for contrast and keyboard access. Do not rely on color alone for risk or status. Keep motion subtle and state-based.
